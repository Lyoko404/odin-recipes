Sour Vegetable Soup Recipe

Description

Ketoprak is a traditional Indonesian dish that uses ketupat as its main ingredient. It's typically sold from pushcarts on the streets or by street vendors . This specialty is commonly found in the Greater Jakarta area and Cirebon City , West Java .

This one is my favorite dish if I am hungry in the night.

Below is the ingredient and steps to make delicious ketoprak:

Ingredients

  • 2 pieces of fried tofu, cut into cubes
  • 100 grams rice vermicelli (glass noodles), soaked in hot water until soft
  • 1 cup bean sprouts, blanched briefly
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 2 rice cakes (lontong), sliced (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • Fried shallots, for topping
  • Crackers (emping or prawn crackers), for serving
  • 4 cloves of garlic

For the peanut sauce

  • 100 grams roasted peanuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 red chilies (adjust to test)
  • 1 or 2 bird's eye chilies (optional, for extra spiciness)
  • 1 teaspoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar
  • Â½ teaspoon salt
  • 100 ml warm water (adjust consistency as needed)

Steps

  1. Step 1: Prepare the peanut sauce

    Grind or blend roasted peanuts, garlic, chilies, tamarind paste, sugar, and salt until smooth. Gradually add warm water and mix until you get a thick but pourable sauce.

  2. Step 2: Prepare the toppings

    Soak the rice vermicelli in hot water until soft, then drain. Blanch the bean sprouts briefly in boiling water. Slice the tofu, rice cake, and cucumber as needed.

  3. Step 3: Assemble the Ketoprak

    In a serving plate or bowl, place rice vermicelli, tofu, bean sprouts, rice cake slices (if using), and cucumber.

  4. Step 4: Add sauce and toppings

    Pour the peanut sauce generously over the top. Drizzle with sweet soy sauce. Sprinkle with fried shallots and serve with crackers on the side.
